I often meet people studying engineering, computer science and similar fields at random STEM-related events. Often, when talking about myself, I mention my website or a blog post, and ask if they have one. A surprising number (probably more than half) don't. Of them, quite a few don't know how to set up a website or why they should do so, so this blog post is intended to clear that up.

My contention is pretty simple: **if you do anything where you could possibly want people to find you online, you should have a website**. For example, if you have a LinkedIn and try to keep it updated so that people can find you, you should also have a website. I think that having a website is useful to you and others in a number of ways, both directly from the website itself and indirectly through the process of building it.

## First: what is a website?

You might be wondering: what do I mean when I say "website"? If you have a LinkedIn profile or public Instagram page, isn't that already a website? Well, kind of. It's true that most people have a page or two on the internet that is dedicated to them and they can edit to say whatever they want, but that's not quite what I'm getting at. When I say website, I mean your own personal website that you construct from the ground up, hosted at a domain[^1] you own and control.

[^1]: A domain is a human-readable name for a website, like `srg.id.au` or `example.com`.

The reason for this is when construct a website yourself rather than inputting information into a pre-existing website like LinkedIn, you get a lot more control. The ways in which this might matter to you are:

- You get more control over your **content**: perhaps you only want to show certain things on your website. For example, you don't want suggested contacts to appear (like LinkedIn) or you *do* want to show your long-form writing and photo gallery at the same time. Most platforms won't let you do this, but you can customise your website however your like.
- You get more control over your **design**: you can choose any design you want, not just what the platform provides. You can express your personality and brand through your website's design.
- You get more control over your **data**: you can choose what to share or not share on your website. You can delete information from your website at any time.
